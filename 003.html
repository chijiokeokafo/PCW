Gallant Cycles Prototype

The owner of Gallant Cycles and YNOT, Tony Mammoliti, has a pretty big name in the Toronto cycling community. It would be a fluke not to see someone on a Gallant Bicycle or wearing YNOT gear and bags when riding in Toronto. Gallant also has a great paint facility. Tony and I spoke about doing some collaboration work after getting a frame painted by him.The idea behind a Gallant Bicycle is you are able to assemble the bike to your liking, choosing from two different frames, and a variety of components and custom colours. They wanted to expand what they were doing by including a third frame into the mix.

This frame had to be able to do everything from cross riding, to fixed gear riding, and everything in between. I had the honour of building the prototype frame for them, so they could advertise and work out any possible kinks in the design. My job was made easy since they already had all the geometry, and an extremely specific idea for everything about the frame design. It had to be simple, but efficient enough to support every type of riding. They incorporated vertical slider dropouts so geared, internal geared, and single speed use was possible. Tony specâ€™d the bike with a 105 gruppo, carbon post, bars and fork so it was practical for city riding, and long road rides. They chose a one off blue/teal colour to top off the bike. This bike shows just how versatile a custom frame can be, and how one bike is actually more than capable to fit all of someones riding needs.
